Challenges Addressed by SignBridge

No Real-Time Communication

Users struggled to have spontaneous conversations between signers and non-signers, leading to social gaps and dependence on interpreters.

Static and Boring Learning

Most tools offered only basic, text-based content—lacking video, animation, or gamification—making progress slow and engagement minimal.

One-Way Translation

Existing apps usually convert sign to text or just text to sign—very few support interactive, bidirectional conversations for real-world needs.

Poor Accessibility Support

Little to no support for users with additional needs (such as visual, cognitive challenges, or multilingual requirements), making access limited.

Lack of Practical Phrases

Daily conversation and essential phrases were missing from most resources, making it hard to use sign language confidently in real-life scenarios.

No Feedback or Motivation

Apps didn’t provide progress tracking, quizzes, or practical feedback—many learners lost momentum early or felt unsupported.
